#Moksha Patam/[मोक्ष पतम्]

Moksha Patam: More than just snakes and ladders.

This 13th-century CE game was designed by Sant Jnaneshwar as a children's game called Moksha Patam [Moksha-Liberation]. Later it became popular by the name of Gyan Chaupar. The other names of this game are: Muktipada or Paramapada.

Historic version of this game had root in morality lessons, where a player's progression up and down the board represented a life journey complicated by virtues [ladders] and vices [Snakes]. It was also associated with traditional Hindu philosophy contrasting karma[कर्म] and kama[काम्], or Destiny[भाग्य] and Desire[इच्छा]. The ladders represented virtues such as generosity, humility and faith, while the snakes represented vices such as lust, anger,theft and murder. The squares representing numbers are symbolic of journey of a human life. Each square represents a positive or negative choice and its following consequence.

The aim of the game is to exit the Vaikuntha [Cosmic consciousness] state, enter this game of life, move through the various planes of consciousness or Lokas, and finally make one's way back to Vaikuntha[Place where lord vishnu resides]. Despite having an aim, this game does not have winners or losers which signifies it more as a journey than a game.

Let us understand the significance, meaning and spiritual message which this interesting game holds.
